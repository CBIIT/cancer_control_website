Rural Cancer Control Meeting
Natcher Conference Center
National Institutes of Health
Bethesda, MD
May 30-31, 2018

Meeting objectives in rural cancer control:

  1. Identify gaps in research and practice
  2. Build partnerships across the country and various health-related entities to address challenges and disseminate solutions
  3. Highlight and identify methods to address competing and common agendas of clinics/providers, researchers and community/patients

The sessions will

  1. incorporate speakers and perspectives from different regions – Delta; Appalachia; Alaska; Hawaii; Northern New England; Southwest; Colonias; Reservation; Frontier
  2. incorporate perspective of participants; community; provider partners in each presentation

May 30

Time Event details
9:00 – 9:20 Opening remarks and meeting charge – Robin Vanderpool (Chair)
9:20 – 9:40 DCCPS mission for the meeting – Bob Croyle, Director DCCPS/NCI
9:40 – 10:00 NCI’s role in promoting rural cancer control - Ned Sharpless, Director, National Cancer Institute
10:00 – 10:20 Building partnerships for cancer control research - Tom Morris, Director,
HRSA Office of Rural Health Policy
10:20 – 10:50 Rural definitions overview and rural demographic patterns – John Cromartie and Michael Ratcliffe  
Goal – Topics to be addressed: Provide an overview of rural definitions and highlight issues related to their use in cancer control research and policy making
10:50 – 11:20 Network break
11:20 – 12:50 Session 1 - Overview of cancer incidence and mortality
Goal: Presentations on the registry data; identifying exposures; and possibilities for data merging to address rural cancer control challenges
12:50 – 2:30 Lunch & poster session (abstract details)
2:30 – 3:30 Session 2 – Methods 
Goal: Discussion of the small population meeting and its relevance to rural populations; how to incorporate social network methods to understand rural populations
3:30 – 4:00 Advocacy and a national perspective – Alan Morgan, CEO, National Rural Health Association
4:00 – 4:15 Break
4:15 – 5:30 Fireside chat  
Goal: Discussion of rural clinics – partnership for research; scope of work, methods

May 31

Time Event details
8:30 – 10:00 Session 3 - Multilevel interventions (impact & policy)
Goal – Lay out challenges and opportunities in community and clinical settings; use of technologies to address gaps in reaching rural populations
10:00 – 10:30 Networking break
10:30 – 11:00 Challenges and solutions to implementation in rural areas – David Chambers, Deputy Director,
Implementation Science, DCCPS/NCI
11:00 – 12:30 Session 4 – Scaling up interventions and building partnerships
Goal:  Highlight challenges in scaling up interventions; aspects to include; how to incorporate rural populations
12:30 – 1:00 Future directions and closing remarks
