Best Practices

Headline Tips

  • Focus on descriptive words and phrases
  • Be specific: Headlines should tell the user what to expect
  • Keep headline length concise â€“ Is every word essential? If not, remove it
  • Google will only show the first 55-60 characters of a page title, so keep it under 55 characters

Examples

Before (100 characters):
“NCI Office of Cancer Survivorship: Celebrating 20 Years of Championing Survivorship Science and Care”

After (55 characters):
“Celebrating 20 Years of Championing Survivorship & Care”

General Copy Tips

  • Use the words your users use
  • Keep content manageable by “chunking” into sections so users can easily scan
  • Write for the user using an active voice
  • Keep the most important information at the top
  • Be clear and brief: use short sentences and paragraphs
  • For content longer than a few paragraphs, break it up into sections with descriptive headings
  • Create clear headings and subheadings that describe the content
  • Avoid using terms and acronyms that are specific to NCI
  • Effectively use white space

Examples

Before:
“This page provides detailed information about currently funded RFA initiatives both led by DCCPS, and those led by other NIH Institutes and Centers (I/Cs) that include DCCPS as a partner. Each initiative includes a table of funded grants and a map that shows the location of funded institutions.”

After:
“View a list of currently funded RFA initiatives led wholly or in part by DCCPS. Learn details about grants awarded in response to each initiative.”

Link Tips

  • Don’t use the phrase, “click here”
  • Do be specific when writing link labels

“Read the Report”
