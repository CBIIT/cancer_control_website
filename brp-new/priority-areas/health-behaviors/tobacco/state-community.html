State and Community Tobacco Control Research Initiative

The National Cancer Institute (NCI) has funded the State and Community Tobacco Control (SCTC) Research Initiative since 2011. Cooperative agreements were awarded to seven research projects and one Coordinating Center. The research conducted by the initiative addresses important, understudied aspects of state and community tobacco control policy and media interventions. The initiative’s four main research areas are:

  • Secondhand smoke policies
  • Tobacco tax and pricing policies
  • Tobacco industry marketing and promotion, and mass media countermeasures
  • Community and social norms

The SCTC Research Web site serves as a public access portal for highlighting SCTC research projects, publications, and products.

2011-2016 NCI-funded SCTC Centers

Research Triangle Institute, Coordinating Center
Staff: Carol Schmitt, Project Director; Todd Rogers, Associate Project Director; and Matthew Farrelly, Senior Advisor

The Coordinating Center is responsible for fostering productive collaboration and communication among the research projects, NCI, and the broader public health community. Coordinating Center researchers and staff provide comprehensive scientific coordination and logistical support for the research projects and NCI to facilitate cross-site research opportunities, evaluation of the initiative, and communication of research findings to states and communities.
