Research Topics: Tobacco Products

Laboratory Assessment of Tobacco Use Behavior and Exposure to Toxins Among Users of New Tobacco Products Promoted to Reduce Harm

The National Cancer Institute awarded a five year Research and Development contract in 2006 to Georgetown University. The purpose of this contract is to develop methods and measures for product testing. The contract will advance scientific knowledge about the toxic and addictive properties of tobacco products marketed by the tobacco industry with claims that imply reduced harm. The contract brings together a consortium of investigators across several institutions.

More information about the Tobacco Product Assessment Consortium (TobPRAC) is available on the project website:
Tobacco Product Assessment Consortium (TobPRAC) Website
