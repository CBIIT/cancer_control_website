Population Health Assessment in Cancer Center Catchment Areas Meeting

  1. Home
  2. Population Health Assessment in Cancer Center Catchment Areas Meeting

The National Cancer Institute’s (NCI) Division of Cancer Control and Population Sciences (DCCPS) is hosting a Population Health Assessment in Cancer Center Catchment Areas Meeting on March 20, 2020, at the Marriott Tucson University Park hotel in Tucson, Arizona.

The goal of the meeting is to share lessons learned related to conceptualizing and conducting cancer center catchment area assessments and gathering local population health data from cancer centers selected to receive P30 administrative supplements as part of the Population Health Assessment in Cancer Center Catchment Areas initiative. The meeting will include presentations of results from the 14 NCI-designated cancer centers funded in Round 2 of the initiative, an update on the Cancer Center Support Grant FOA, and a poster session where all cancer centers can highlight work related to catchment area assessment.

Submit a Poster Abstract

The deadline for submitting a poster abstract is 11:59 PM ET, Friday, January 10, 2020.

Call for Poster Abstracts: Poster abstracts are being solicited to highlight the methods and findings of efforts to define and describe the catchment area populations of U.S. cancer centers. Submissions are not limited to NCI-designated cancer centers or P30 administrative supplement awardees. Grantees who were awarded 2018 P30 administrative supplements for catchment area assessments do not need to submit an abstract for their oral presentation/main study, but junior investigators and co-investigators on those supplement grants are welcome to submit poster abstracts that highlight other uses of the survey data. Round 1 (2016-2017) P30 supplement grantees are encouraged to submit posters as a follow up to their presentations given at the 2018 cancer center catchment area grantee meeting at Albert Einstein Cancer Center in New York. Community groups and organizations who are partnering with cancer centers to use the data to inform population health activities are also welcome to submit poster abstracts to highlight their work.
