Linda D. Cameron, PhD

Research Interests
Linda D. Cameron, PhD, is Professor of Psychology at University of California, Merced.

Dr. Cameronâ€™s research focuses on self-regulation processes influencing health and illness behavior. She is particularly interested in using the Common-Sense Model of illness cognition to evaluate the representations and emotion regulation processes guiding responses to health threat information. One line of research involves the development of measures assessing both conceptual and imagery contents of illness risk representations. Dr. Cameron and colleagues are using these measures to explore beliefs and imagery relating to cancer risk and their influence on motivations to engage in protective behavior. Another line of research explores affective and imagery processes influencing responses to health communications, including messages about genetic testing and illness risk. These findings are applied in the development of imagery-based interventions for communicating health risk information. Dr. Cameron is also interested in exploring the influences of anxiety and emotion regulation strategies on symptom perception, use of health care services, and adjustment to cancer and other chronic illnesses. These findings inform her research on developing interventions for helping individuals cope with cancer treatment and manage worries about cancer recurrence.
