The Cognitive, Affective, and Social Processes in Health Research (CASPHR) Workgroup

CASPHR Members



David Portnoy, PhD, MPH

Research Interests
David Portnoy, PhD, MPH is a Cancer Prevention Fellow in the Behavioral Research Program at the NCI.

Dr. Portnoy’s research interests focus broadly on health risk perception, attitudes and decision making. His research uses psychological theory and methods on such health topics as HIV prevention, nutrition promotion and cancer control and prevention. Specifically, he has investigated barriers to effective doctor-patient communication such as low numeracy in genetic testing encounters and how waiting for medical test results can limit patients’ ability to participate in shared decision making, and currently is working on a number of projects focused on the role of uncertainty on the health cognitions of doctors and the public. A second line of work focuses on health risk perception; as it can drive or inhibit preventive behavior in healthy individuals, affect health attitudes and practices in members of families at high risk for genetically-linked diseases, as well as methods to most effectively change risk perceptions without affecting other psychological constructs. He has additional research interests in the use of technology, including virtual reality and computer-based behavioral health interventions, for the measurement of health attitudes and behaviors as well as for health behavior change.

Selected Affiliations
Dr. Portnoy has served as a reviewer for a number of journals at the intersection of psychology and health, as well as for grant and research competitions for graduate students and post-doctoral fellows. During graduate school he was a member of the Society for Personality and Social Psychology (SPSP) graduate student committee and a campus representative and student mentor for the Association for Psychological Science (APS). He is currently a research affiliate of the Center for Health, Intervention, and Prevention at the University of Connecticut.
