Health Communication and Informatics Research: Research Areas
Health Literacy

HCIRB supports research on health literacy, defined as “the degree to which individuals can obtain, process, and understand the basic health information and services they need to make appropriate health decisions” (Institute of Medicine, 2004). Â Limited health literacy is an important contributor to health disparities and frequently a barrier to medical screening, treatment, health-related quality of life, and cancer communication. In partnership with eleven other NIH institutes and centers, NCI has supported health literacy funding opportunities since 2004: investigators are encouraged to submit applications in response to PAR-13-130 (R01), PAR-13-131 (R03), and PAR-13-132 (R21). Please refer to these funding opportunity announcements for details on scientific priorities. NCI’s specific areas of interest include:

  • Multilevel interventions, particularly research initiatives which seek to address health literacy at a health systems level
  • Testing new communication strategies and channels, including information technologies and Web 2.0/social media
  • Identification of how populations access and use health information technology across varying health literacy levels

For more information about health literacy research at NCI, please contact Wen-ying Sylvia Chou, Program Director, at 240-276-6954 or chouws@mail.nih.gov. Examples of projects which were recently funded through this PAR are provided below.
