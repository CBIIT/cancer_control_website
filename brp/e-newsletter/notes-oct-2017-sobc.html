  • Announcement: Funding opportunities for NIH's Science of Behavior Change (SOBC) Common Fund Program

NIH’s Common Fund for Science of Behavior Change (SOBC) promotes basic research across disciplines on the initiation, personalization, and maintenance of behavior change. SOBC focuses on mechanisms of experimental medicine to:

  1. identify an intervention target,
  2. develop assays (measures) to permit verification of the target,
  3. engage the target through experimentation or intervention, and
  4. test the degree to which engagement produces the desired behavior change.

Current Funding Opportunities

  • SOBC: Revision Applications for Use-inspired Research to Optimize Adherence, Behavior Change Interventions, and Outcomes (R01) (RFA-RM-17-022)
  • SOBC: Revision Applications for Use-inspired Research to Optimize Adherence, Behavior Change Interventions, and Outcomes (U01) (RFA-RM-17-023)
  • SOBC: Revision Applications for Use-inspired Research to Optimize Adherence, Behavior Change Interventions, and Outcomes (R34) (RFA-RM-17-024)
  • SOBC: Use-inspired Research to Optimize Adherence, Behavior Change Interventions, and Outcomes (R21) (RFA-RM-17-028)

SOBC Measures Repository Applicants are required to use at least one SOBC Research Network assay. The assays are accessible through the SOBC Measures Repository. For questions or technical assistance, contact the SOBC Resource and Coordinating Center: info@scienceofbehaviorchange.org. Pre-application Technical Assistance Webinars https://commonfund.nih.gov/behaviorchange

  • Thursday, October 26, 2017, 1 p.m. ET and
  • Monday, October 30, 2017, 12 p.m. ET

The NIH Common Fund is managed by the NIH Office of the Director in partnership with several NIH Institutes, Centers, and Offices, including the National Cancer Institute. Additional behavioral research-associated funding announcements are available online at cancercontrol.cancer.gov/brpfunding.
