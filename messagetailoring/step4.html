This is Step 4: Inserting Merge Fields into the Main document

First an overview of the tools used to tailor a document

Selecting the Mail Merge Helper will display the Mail Merge Toolbar. We use three additional buttons to tailor documents. The Show/Hide button, which displays non-printing characters like returns and spaces, the View Field Codes and the Update Field buttons.
 The Show/Hide button. This button may be on your toolbar, or it may have to be placed in your toolbar.
 The Update Fields button. This button has to be added manually.
 The Show Field Codes button. This button has to be added manually.


Activate the Show/Hide button using the Options choice in the Tools menu.
The Update Field Codes button and the Show Field Codes button can be placed on the Main toolbar by the following method: Under Tools, select Customize. The Customize dialog box below will appear (Left).

Click the Commands tab and scroll to All Commands on the left. On the right, scroll down to find the appropriate button icons. Make sure the Save In menu reads Normal (Mac OS) or Normal.dot (Windows). Click and hold on the button icon and a small "x" will appear by your cursor. Drag the "x" up to your main tool bar, positioning it so that a vertical line appears on the toolbar, and release. Do this for each of the buttons listed. When finished, click the Close button at the lower right.

Now to insert a merge field.

Let's start with the address of your recipient. Your letter can be automatically addressed to the individual receiving it by inserting a merge field for each line of an address. As a first step, turn on the View Field Codes button. This will allow you to see the merge fields. Next, place your cursor where the information should appear in the letter.

Click Insert Merge Field from the Merge Field toolbar. Your field names from your database will appear in a drop-down menu. Choose "Fname" as the first item of the address (Left).

Insert another merge field for the last name in the same way, leaving a space between the two merge fields. Continue to Inserting Merge Fields for all lines of the address and for the salutation.

Note that spaces and punctuation placed outside the curly braces of a merge field will appear in every letter. On the other hand, spaces within the curly braces are part of the code and do not affect the merged data. Can you see the space between the first name and the last name?

You will find it helpful to turn on the Show/Hide button so that you can see where the spaces and returns occur in your document. When the letters are generated, each row (or record) in your database will provide the information for one letter. Your Main document containing merge fields should look like Figure 10. When you deselect the View Field Codes button, you will see the field names within double chevrons (Fig. 11). The chevrons act like as placeholders.
