    • controlled environment / genetic strain in animal studies

    • can more readily rerun a failed project with animals, though some cost is involved

  8. Criteria For Defining a Causal Relationship
    • Koch (infectious agent is the cause of a disease)
    • the organism is present in each case
    • the organism can be grown in pure culture
    • the organism precipitates the disease in inoculated, susceptible animals
    • temporal relationship between cause and effect
    • strength of the association: (a) clinical significance, (b) statistical significance (c) dose-response relationships
    • reversible association
    • consistency
    • biologic plausibility

  11. No Study Is Perfect
    • But in the end we want to make some statement about the amount of contribution that the study makes to science
    • Poor quality studies contribute little.
