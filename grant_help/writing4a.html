Discussion

The benefits of conducting high quality research are obvious: The field advances from good science, and because the research is presumably timely, the field may benefit from a single research project for many years. On a more personal level, the investigators enjoy the satisfaction of producing high quality work, and may reap the benefits of their work in the form of citations, invited lectures, new opportunities, and so on for many years to come. Eventually, poor quality research is recognized as such first by other researchers in the field and then by others who apply information from the research. If an inferior study is cited at all, usually the citations occur soon after the paper is published, or the study is used as an example of how not to conduct research.

Becoming knowledgeable about research methodology is logical not only for researchers, but also for those who utilize the results of research papers. Understanding the tenets of research design better enables one to:

  • Evaluate studies
  • Design studies
  • Select effective treatment strategies from others' research
  • Develop institutional tools, lectures, and academic programs
  • Plan and effect policy
  • Increase clinical skills and treatment efficacy
  • Obtain grant funding
  • Publish high quality papers

Evidence from at least a few studies is usually required to counter a single study with a positive result. If the study with a positive result is one in which the design is weak, much time and research money will be wasted to refute questionable findings.

Second, if the results are published from a weak study in which some intervention or therapy is promoted, the target populations may be exposed to treatments that are costly but ineffective.
