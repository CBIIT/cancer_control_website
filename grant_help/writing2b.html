The Grant Writing Process - Budgeting

Budget Preparation - PHS 398

The budgets required as part of the PHS 398 Grant Application include a detailed budget of the first 12-month period and a summary of the costs for the entire budget period. Proper description and justification of most cost items are also required and may be included on continuation pages. It is recommended that detailed budgets be prepared for all years of the project. This will prove helpful in not only justifying the costs but also managing the funds when the research is underway. In addition, due to budget cuts at the funding agency, there may be years when the research project is not fully funded. These detailed yearly budgets will help determining where costs may be cut without substantially affecting the research.

On the two budget pages of the PHS 398 Grant Application, the PI is required to itemize and justify direct costs for a number of different cost categories, including: personnel, consultants, equipment, supplies, travel, patient care, alternations and renovations, consortium/contracts, and other expenses. The determinations of these costs may be quite involved. For example, to determine personnel costs, the PI must identify the personnel who will work on the project, their roles on the project, their salaries and fringe benefits, their type of appointment, the effort on the project, and finally anticipate any future salary increases or personnel changes. Most of the administrative information (salaries and fringe benefits) may be obtained from the organization's office of sponsored research. The use of consultants should also be considered and may provide expertise in some area of research (e.g., statistical sampling) that the proposed project team may be lacking, or provide a particular service for which personnel can not be obtained at the PIs organizations. The PI must be careful not to farm too much of the work out to consultants. If most of the work is performed by consultants, reviewers may question the overall competence of the PI and the research team.

All other direct costs must be arranged into the cost categories on the budget forms. (See the instructions in the PHS 398 Grant Application kit for more information about the specific cost categories.) The costs must also be allowable. Some costs, such as depreciation, may not be allowable as direct costs. For a definition of possible project costs and a discussion of allowable and unallowable costs, the PI should consult the PHS Grants Policy Statement.

The PHS Grants Policy Statement also discusses indirect costs and how to establish an indirect cost rate. Indirect costs are costs which can not be identified specifically for individual projects or programs. These costs are entered onto the Checklist page of the PHS 398 Grant Application and may include such things as facilities operation and administrative expenses. The PIs organization incurs these costs and must have negotiated an indirect cost rate with the particular Federal agency for which the grant is being submitted.

A direct cost is any cost that can be specifically identified with a particular project or program. Direct costs may include, but not be limited to, salaries, travel, equipment, and supplies directly benefitting the project or activity.

On the PHS 398 Grant Applications direct costs are entered onto page DD for the initial budget period and page EE for the entire proposed period.

The following is a list of possible direct costs which may be incorporated into the budget. Click on the word or phrase you would like to obtain more information.

Personnel Costs

  • Project Personnel
  • Type Of Appointment
  • Percentage Of Appointment
  • Effort On The Project
  • Salary
  • Fringe Benefits

Non-Personnel Costs

  • Supply Costs
  • Consultant Costs
  • Travel Costs
  • Patient Care Costs
  • Alterations and Renovations
  • Consortium/Contractual Costs
  • Other Expenses
