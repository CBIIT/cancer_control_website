Strategies to Prevent or Remediate Cancer- and Treatment-Associated Aging

Monday, February 11, 2019
National Cancer Institute Shady Grove Campus

Tuesday, February 12, 2019
Johns Hopkins University Montgomery County Campus


The Cancer & Accelerated Aging Initiative seeks to identify scientific research needs at the intersection of cancer and aging, encourage integration of aging-related endpoints into cancer treatment and survivorship studies, and build a transdisciplinary research portfolio to optimize healthy aging for cancer survivors.

At the think tank meeting “Strategies to Prevent or Remediate Cancer- and Treatment-Associated Aging,” the second in the series for this initiative, we will strive to elucidate intervention approaches that prevent or mitigate the short- and long-term consequences of cancer and cancer treatment. Through this work we aim to identify areas of scientific research need and to produce a strategic and unified vision to further our research agenda.
