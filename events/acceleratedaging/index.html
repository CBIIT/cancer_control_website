Measuring Aging and Identifying Aging Phenotypes in Cancer Survivors

July 25, 2018
National Cancer Institute
9609 Medical Center Drive
Rockville, MD 20850
July 26, 2018
Johns Hopkins University
9601 Medical Center Drive
Rockville, MD 20850

This think tank meeting is the first in a series of deliberations for the Cancer & Accelerated Aging Initiative to identify research gaps and promising approaches to improve our ability to understand, predict, and mitigate short- and long-term aging-related consequences of cancer and cancer treatment.

Through our efforts, we ultimately seek to identify scientific and research needs, encourage integration of aging-related endpoints into cancer treatment and survivorship studies, and build a transdisciplinary research portfolio to optimize healthy aging for cancer survivors. We will strive to elucidate the best methods to measure aging among cancer survivors, identify aging phenotypes, and produce a strategic and unified vision to fill the identified research gaps and establish methodological approaches to characterize aging phenotypes.
