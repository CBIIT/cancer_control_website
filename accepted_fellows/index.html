Forms for CRTAs to Accept the Fellowship

This page is for individuals who have received official acceptance into a Cancer Research Training Award (CRTA) fellowship within the Division of Cancer Control and Population Sciences (DCCPS) at the National Cancer Institute (NCI). CRTA fellows collaborate, train, and work alongside scientific and programmatic staff.

As you consider this offer, please be aware of the following distinctions in this position:

  • CRTA fellows are not federal employees.
  • Payment is disbursed on the first of the month for the previous month worked.
  • Personal and vacation time does not accrue. However, fellows can request excused absences at the discretion of their supervisor.
  • For tax purposes, CRTAs will receive a 1099 rather than a W2.
    • Fellows are responsible for paying quarterly taxes because taxes will not be withheld from the monthly stipend. Fellows will be responsible for paying state and federal taxes (see provisions agreement).
  • Fellows may request a small relocation allowance. Approval is based upon current budget restrictions and supervisor discretion.

In order to accept the CRTA offer, several steps must be completed.  Respond to your offer email with the following completed forms attached. If you have questions, please contact the DCCPS Fellowship Training Coordinator, Richard P. Moser, at moserr@mail.nih.gov.

A. Checklist of required forms to activate the fellowship award

If you have already submitted a form on this list, you do not need to send it again.

  1. Detailed resume/CV (please include all educational and relevant work experience)
  2. Two signed letters of reference on letterhead
  3. Self-Identification of Handicap (SF 256)
  4. Race and National Origin Identification Ethnicity and Race Identification (SF 181)
  5. Provisions Agreement (PDF)
  6. Insurance Application (PDF) or Insurance Waiver (PDF)

    A note about health care: Under the terms of the fellowship, CRTAs must be covered by adequate health insurance. We provide Foundation for Advanced Education in the Sciences (FAES) insurance free of charge through Aetna. For more information, visit: https://faes.org/content/health-insurance-services . The FAES insurance election must be indicated using the form below, or a waiver should be submitted to decline coverage. Fellows may also elect to purchase dental and/or vision insurance at their own expense through FAES.

  7. Automated Clearing House (ACH) Payment Enrollment (PDF)
    Complete the second section ONLY (Payee Information); provide a signature in the Contact Field, and attach a voided personal check.
  8. Approved Training Plan
    You should have received a training plan with your acceptance letter. Please work with your mentor to finalize this plan, then review, sign, and return. (If you have not yet received your training plan, please contact Richard Moser at moserr@mail.nih.gov).
  9. Proof of Citizenship
    Photocopy of birth certificate or passport
  10. Official transcripts and/or proof of academic good standing
    Send transcript of highest degree conferred. If currently enrolled in school, proof of academic good standing must be sent on official letterhead and signed by graduate program director, adviser, or equivalent.
  11. Cover letter or brief statement
    Please tell us about your current course of study, your academic interests, why you want to participate in the CRTA fellowship program, and how you hope this experience will help your career.

Please review all forms carefully before sending. Any omissions can delay the processing of your paperwork. You may fax, mail hard copies, or email scanned copies of these documents in a reply email to your acceptance letter. Emails containing sensitive or personally identifiable information should be avoided.

B. Online training to complete before your fellowship begins

Once we receive all required information, you will be entered into our NIH Employee Database (NED). At that point you will receive an email from NED, and you will be issued an NIH Identification Number. You should then complete two mandatory online computer security trainings (http://irtsectraining.nih.gov/). You will need your NIH ID# for this training. It is best to complete the trainings before you arrive for your fellowship.

  • Information Security Awareness (< 2 hours)
  • Privacy Awareness Course (< 2 hours)

Note: After your information is entered in NED, you will need to complete the Office of Personnel Management’s Electronic Questionnaires for Investigations Processing (e-QIP) to electronically enter, update, and transmit your personal data to NIH over a secure Internet connection. You should receive an email from the Office of Research Services to begin this process within two business days. If you don’t, call 301-402-9755 or email ORSequip@mail.nih.gov. This e-QIP email is an important step toward receiving your security badge, which gives you access to the building. Please respond to this email as soon as you can. If you don’t, your start date could be affected.

You also will receive a separate email to set up an appointment to initiate your security badge application. A second appointment will be required to pick up your badge.

C. Fellowship contacts and helpful information

Please stay in close contact with your supervisor and/or mentor as the start date approaches to ensure the proper paperwork has been received.

Current fellows are available to answer questions and provide information about their fellowship experiences. Contact Richard Moser if you would like to be connected to a current fellow:  moserr@mail.nih.gov. 

Most fellowships are located at the NCI Shady Grove campus, which is easily accessible via public transportation or personal vehicle. NCI offers a free shuttle between the Shady Grove metro station and the Shady Grove campus throughout the day. A shuttle also runs between the NIH campus in Bethesda and NCI campus. The shuttle is wheelchair accessible and runs every 15 minutes (PDF). Housing rentals are often posted on http://nih.samslist.us/ and http://www.fedesp.com, among other sites. Please note that NCI cannot endorse any rental listing or website.
