About Cancer Survivorship Research: Message from the Director

Welcome to the Office of Cancer Survivorship Web site. The office was created to support research that addresses the short- and long-term physical, social and psychological effects of cancer and its treatment on survivors and their families. Advances achieved in the last part of the 20th century in the way cancer is diagnosed and treated have led to more people being cured of or living long periods of time free of their disease. There are over 8 million cancer survivors in the United States alone. The Office of Cancer Survivorship reflects the National Cancer Institute's commitment to provide the most current information about and the best methods to monitor and care for this growing population of cancer survivors.

If this is your first visit to the Office of Cancer Survivorship Web site, please tour our site. For cancer survivors and their families, this site offers up-to-date information about what's new in survivorship (e.g., resource materials and trends). For researchers and practitioners, this site provides valuable information about the OCS's scientific portfolio, research priorities and opportunities for training and research funding in the area of cancer survivorship.

The dawning of a new millenium brings us rich opportunities not only to bring greater awareness to the unique needs of survivors, but also to use the personal and collective experiences of survivors to inform the National Cancer Institute's efforts to reduce the burden of cancer for all. I appreciate you visiting our Web site and hope that you will come away with new knowledge and perhaps be inspired to add your own contribution to advancing the mission of the Office of Cancetr Survivorship.


Julia H. Rowland, Ph.D.
Director, Office of Cancer Survivorship
