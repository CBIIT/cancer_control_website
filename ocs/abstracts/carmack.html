Effect of Prostate Cancer on Spousal Relationships & Quality of Life
Cindy Carmack, PhD

The proposed study has three phases. The first is to conduct interviews with a total of thirty spouses of prostate cancer patients and generate questionnaire items for an intimacy measure. The second phase is to pilot test the questionnaire with a representative sample of fifty spouses and conduct refinements as needed. The final phase is to assess spouses' quality of life (QOL) including the newly developed Intimacy Questionnaire through a cross-sectional study with 300 spouses. A total of 380 spouses of prostate cancer patients will be recruited from three M.D. Anderson clinics, and three funded studies in which patient's receive their care at other medical facilities. Preliminary studies on QOL conducted by a multidisciplinary team from the University of Texas M.D. Anderson Cancer Center provide the context for this project. Project results will inform a spousal QOL model developed by the investigators based on prior studies.

The specific objectives of the project are to: (1) develop a measure that assesses aspects of relationship intimacy that may be affected by prostate cancer; (2) characterize the QOL and adjustment of the spouses of men with prostate cancer; (3) examine the effects of patient QOL on spouse QOL; and (4) examine the mediating effects of the quality of the relationship, coping style, social support, disease stage, and type of treatment on the QOL of spouses of men with prostate cancer. It is hypothesized that the patients' QOL will be related to the spouses' QOL and that the quality of the spousal relationship, including sexual relations, intimacy, communication patterns, and marital distress, will mediate the effect the patients' QOL has on the QOL of their spouses.
