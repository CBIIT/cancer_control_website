Multiple Family Groups in Head & Neck Cancer
Jamie Ostroff, PhD

The proposed study has two phases. The first is to conduct a cross-sectional survey of the needs and concerns of recently diagnosed head and neck cancer patients and family caregivers. The second phase is to develop and pilot test the efficacy of a modified one-day multiple family group intervention with a subset of randomly chosen survey respondents from phase one (sample size unspecified). The investigators' experience applying the 6-week multiple family group intervention provides the context for the modified one-day intervention. The proposed study examines the impact of the intervention on multiple domains of psychosocial adaptation at two points in time--- baseline and one week post intervention. The goals of the project as related to families of head and neck patients are to: (1) examine the impact of head and neck cancer on family members; and 2) examine interest, barriers and short-term effects of the multiple family group intervention.
