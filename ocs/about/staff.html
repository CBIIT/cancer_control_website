Health-related Quality of Life

Health-related quality of life (HRQoL) focuses on the effect of health on people's well-being, including their physical, mental, emotional, and social functioning. Examples of work in this area are studies that assess ways to measure HRQoL.

Patterns & Quality of Care

Patterns and quality of care focus on the health-related care received by survivors, and the extent to which their care follows nationally recognized guidelines. This research includes, for example, survivorship care planning, patient-provider communication, adherence to treatment, insurance coverage, access to care, risk perception, and the use of complementary and alternative medicine.

Research Tools & Platforms

Research platforms are networks or technology that enable the creation of products and processes that support present or future development. Some examples include survivor cohorts (groups of survivors that are examined over time), studies that use databases such as NCI's Surveillance, Epidemiology, and End Results (SEER) Program, and the testing of new technology designed to help survivors make medical decisions.
