National Cancer Institute, Office of Cancer Survivorship Position Available

The National Cancer Institute (NCI), a major research component of the National Institutes of Health (NIH) and Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS), is seeking a dynamic medical, behavioral or social scientist to join the staff of the Office of Cancer Survivorship (OCS). We are looking for a person with an established record of research, publications, and projects relevant to cancer survivorship in particular, or cancer prevention and control in general. We are interested in candidates who:

  • Have strong expertise in the methods and practice of medical or behavioral science research in cancer control,
  • Can effectively manage research grants and contracts,
  • Can conduct important independent and collaborative cancer survivorship and health-related quality of life research and publication projects,
  • Have excellent writing and communication skills,
  • Can represent the NCI and articulate the goals and achievements of the OCS in a broad range of forums
  • Can help coordinate a national program of grant-funded research in cancer survivorship and identify new areas and initiatives that will advance the art and science of cancer survivorship research and care.

The OCS was founded by the NCI in recognition of the growing population of individuals living long-term following a diagnosis of cancer and the need to identify and address the unique challenges facing these cancer survivors and their family members. The importance of cancer survivorship to the mission of the NCI is highlighted in its plan for investment (http://plan2004.cancer.gov/). The OCS supports a diverse portfolio of grants and is involved in a number of exciting research as well as educational outreach projects some of which are detailed on the OCS web site (http://survivorship.cancer.gov). This position offers an excellent opportunity for an individual interested in setting the national agenda for the study, development and application of cancer survivorship research and clinical care.

For additional information contact: Gail Murphy, NIH Human Resources Office, 301-402-5530. The NCI vacancy announcement for this position contains complete application procedures and lists all mandatory information which you must submit with your application. To obtain the vacancy announcement for this position which will be posted under announcement # NCI-03-2109, you may visit the NIH Career website at: http://careerhere.nih.gov/ OR you can have it faxed to you by calling 1-800-728-JOBS (for local calls, 301-594-2953) and enter the “FAX ID Number” 1906. You will be prompted for your fax machine number. Submit applications through CareerHere or to DHRO Operations Branch A, 6120 Executive Blvd Suite 519, Rockville, MD 20852. Applications must be postmarked by 09/02/03.

Salary will be within the Civil Service range for a GS-14 ($80,690 to $104,900 annually). Highly qualified candidates will have doctoral level training and experience in behavioral or social sciences, public health, medicine, epidemiology or in other relevant health sciences areas. The NIH and DHHS are equal opportunity employers.
