2nd Biennial Cancer Survivorship Research Conference

Save the Date: The National Cancer Institute and The American Cancer Society are pleased to announce that the 2nd biennial Cancer Survivorship Research Conference will be conducted June 16-18, 2004 in Washington DC. Please save these dates on your calendar and forward this message to all interested colleagues. We apologize in advance if you receive multiple notices regarding this meeting. To promote wide participation for this event we have conducted broad outreach including postings with multiple listserves.

The title for this year’s conference is Cancer Survivorship: Pathways to Health After Treatment. The focus will be on the health, well-being & surveillance of survivors after cancer'. Key outcomes for the conference include identifying:

  1. the long-term and late effects of cancer and its treatment;
  2. populations at greatest risk for these effects;
  3. interventions, behaviors and surveillance efforts designed to improve health outcomes and survivor and family well-being; and
  4. strategies and resources to support and implement change at the research, clinical, and policy level.

More details regarding the conference will follow in the coming weeks. We look forward to a stimulating conference focused on the continued advancement of cancer survivorship research.
